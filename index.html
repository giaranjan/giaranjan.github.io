Gia Ranjan


I am a ML Engineer by profession based at the World Bank in Washington, D.C. where I work on causal and multimodal AI.
Additionally, I conduct research at the Guy’s and St. Thomas Hospital in London where I am currently building gaussian regression models for differentiating the brain metrics of healthy infants from those with congenital heart disease (CHD).
On a more personal note, I am fascinated by things that I don’t understand. Dismantling them to see their inner workings makes me happy!
Fun fact: I started my career as a diplomat/ economist working in international policy and negotiations before transitioning into computer science, machine learning and engineering.

Research Interests

Primary Interests: machine learning, multimodal deep learning, systems engineering, full stack engineering for machine learning, chaotic and stochastic systems

Background and Past Experiences

I completed my BSc in Economics at King's College London focused on computation, monetary policy, and international trade/finance. Primarily I researched under Dr. David Aikman (of US Federal Reserve) on macroprudential policies and causal impacts of interest rates and credit controls in the era of UK Radcliffe Report. During this time I had a stint at McKinsey and Company in London.

I then worked on multiple projects with the UN. I interned with the UN in Tanzania with Federica Margini and Diego Angemi where I conducted research on policy outcomes. I subsequently worked with the BRGM in Indonesia on a pilot implementation in the peatlands of Riau and Central Kalimantan to restore peatlands destroyed by Asian haze.

In my final year, I worked with the UN in DRC under Thomas Maketa Lutete where I designed new economic models to tackle market failure in the DRC cocoa value chain. The goal was to restructure the entire value chain for the commodity in the country.

Finally, I was a predoctorate under Professor George Loewenstein at Carnegie Mellon University, and then a Masters student in Computer Science at Georgia Tech.
