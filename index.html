AI scientist, python developer and (in a past life) economist. Sometimes I do art.

I am an AI scientist and (former) economist currently working at Lockheed Martin. I also conduct field research under Dr. George Loewenstein.

Prior to this, I studied Economics at King's College London and undertook several research projects as an undergraduate. I was a research assistant with David Aikman, Oliver Bush and Alan Taylor working on macroprudential policies and causal impacts of interest rates and credit controls in the era of UK Radcliffe Report . Following this, I undertook field research for the UN in Tanzania with Federica Margini and Diego Angemi, where I studied, analysed and experimented on 3 topics in social and economic policy - mobile money transaction in sub-Saharan Africa, urban-rural divide in health outcomes in Tanzania (conducted for Institute of Tropical Medicine Antwerp) and extractive industry fiscal policies. In my final year as an undergraduate, I completed a field internship with the UN in DRC under Thomas Maketa Lutete where I designed new economic models to tackle market failure in the DRC cocoa value chain.
