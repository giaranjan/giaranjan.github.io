Gia Ranjan


I am a machine learning engineer and economist (in that order) currently working at a startup. I am also a predoc for Dr. George Loewenstein at Carnegie Mellon University. In my free time, I volunteer at the AI for Good Foundation developing the sentiment model for their Trendscanner Project.

My research interest is in using AI and computation to solve problems in natural and economic sciences. I am interested in how the world works, why it works the way it does, and if it is possible to influence/ manipulate/ disrupt the dynamics of these workings/ systems.

On a more personal note, I am fascinated by things that I don’t understand and the idea of dismantling their inner workings. When I am not working, you can find me engrossed in martial arts, painting, philosophy or working for my helicopter license.

I have my own non profit called Meliora and it is currently assisting rural folk painters in areas around the India - Nepal - China border.

Background and Past Experiences

I finished my BSc in Economics at King's College London. My work during undergrad focussed at the intersection of computation, economics and policy. I was an RA under David Aikman, Oliver Bush and Alan Taylor working on macroprudential policies and causal impacts of interest rates and credit controls in the era of UK Radcliffe Report.


I was a research intern for the United Nations where I undertook field research in Tanzania with Federica Margini and Diego Angemi. Additionally, I assisted the BRGM in Indonesia on a pilot implementation in the peatland landscapes of Riau and Central Kalimantan restoring the peatlands destroyed in the asian hazes.


In my final year, I completed an internship with the UN in DRC under Thomas Maketa Lutete where I designed new economic models to tackle market failure in the DRC cocoa value chain. My work sought to restructure the entire value chain for the commodity in the country (and, by extension, in west Africa).
