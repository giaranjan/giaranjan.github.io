Gia Ranjan


I am a ML Engineer and Economist - in that order. I am based at the World Bank in Washington, D.C. where I work on causal and multimodal AI as a Data Scientist.

I also conduct research at the Guy’s and St. Thomas Hospital in London where I am currently building gaussian regression models for differentiating the brain metrics of healthy infants from those with congenital heart disease (CHD). In my free time, I volunteer at the AI for Good Foundation developing the sentiment model for their Trendscanner Project.

On a more personal note, I am fascinated by things that I don’t understand. Dismantling them to see their inner workings makes me happy.

Background and Past Experiences


I finished my BSc in Economics at King's College London. My work during undergrad focussed on computation, economics and policy. I was an RA under Bank of England’s Dr. David Aikman working on macroprudential policies and causal impacts of interest rates and credit controls in the era of UK Radcliffe Report.


I then interned with the UN where I undertook field research in Tanzania with Federica Margini and Diego Angemi. Additionally, I assisted the BRGM in Indonesia on a pilot implementation in the peatland landscapes of Riau and Central Kalimantan to restore the peatlands destroyed by Asian haze.


In my final year, I worked with the UN in DRC under Thomas Maketa Lutete where I designed new economic models to tackle market failure in the DRC cocoa value chain. My work sought to restructure the entire value chain for the commodity in the country (and, by extension, in west Africa).
